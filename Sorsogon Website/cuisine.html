Cuisine of Sorsogon

Food Culture

Coconut, chili, and the sea

Sorsoganon cuisine is defined by bold coconut milk (gata), fiery labuyo chilis, and the freshest seafood from the surrounding Pacific and Sorsogon Bay. As part of the Bicol region, it shares in a culinary tradition celebrated across the Philippines for its depth and heat.

Fermented shrimp paste (bagoong na alamang) and smoked fish from coastal communities give Sorsogon's food its own distinctly local character — rich, complex, and deeply satisfying.

Must-Try

Signature dishes

Kinunot na Pating

Sorsogon's most iconic dish — shredded shark meat in rich coconut milk with labuyo chilis and malunggay leaves.

Laing

Dried taro leaves slow-cooked in coconut milk with shrimp paste and chili. Smoky, savory, deeply satisfying.

Pinangat

Taro leaves stuffed with fish, wrapped into packets, and simmered in coconut milk with ginger and chili.

Bicol Express

Diced pork in coconut milk, bagoong, and abundant chili — the dish that made Bikol cooking famous nationwide.

Sinantolan

Santol fruit in coconut milk with shrimp paste — a unique sweet-sour-savory side dish of the Bicol region.

Pili Nut Sweets

Candies, pastillas, and pralines made from the native pili nut — the most beloved pasalubong of the province.

Bagoong na Alamang

Fermented shrimp paste — an indispensable condiment in Sorsoganon cooking, served alongside almost everything.

Buko Juice

Fresh young coconut water, served cold at markets and roadside stalls — the ultimate tropical refreshment.
