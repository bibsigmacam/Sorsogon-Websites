Faith & Colonial Architecture

Churches of Sorsogon

A Province of Faith

Four centuries of Catholic heritage

Spanish missionaries established parishes across Sorsogon beginning in the late 16th century, constructing churches that blended Baroque architecture with local materials and craftsmanship.

Many survive as both living places of worship and testaments to provincial heritage. Several are adorned with original colonial-era art, altars, and artifacts lovingly preserved by local communities for generations.

Heritage Structures

Notable churches

Sorsogon City

St. John the Baptist Parish (Bacon Church)

One of the finest examples of colonial ecclesiastical architecture in the province. Its restored coral stone facade and bell tower date to the 18th century, overlooking Sorsogon Bay.

Sorsogon City

Saints Peter & Paul Cathedral

The mother church of the Diocese of Sorsogon — the most prominent religious structure in the capital, focal point of all major liturgical celebrations.

Bulusan

St. John the Baptist — Bulusan

A charming stone church backdropped by the volcano that shares the town's name. The churchyard offers one of the most photographed views in the province.

Gubat

St. Francis Xavier Parish — Gubat

An elegant 19th-century church in the coastal town of Gubat, dedicated to the Jesuit missionary who traveled through Southeast Asia in the 1540s.
